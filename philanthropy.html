Philanthropy Strategy & Impact Advisory

Robin advises foundations, family offices, and social impact funds, giving them confidence that their donations are shaping systems, not just treating symptoms.

The practice helps clients decide where to give to create the biggest impact, and measures whether their giving is making a difference.

•Approach

Grounded in expertise in economics and public policy, this work brings analytical and quantitative rigour to large-scale philanthropy. For more than a decade, Robin has conducted in-depth research and advisory work across academia, leading think tanks, and the charitable and public sectors.

This work has focused on complex societal challenges including poverty and inequality, economic inclusion, reconciliation, and climate transition. This experience provides a research-backed understanding of how systems function and how they can be changed.

The result is disciplined philanthropy: giving with intention, guided by evidence, and evaluated against outcomes that matter.

•Three core pillars

Pillar I

Strategy design

  • Clarifying the donor's mission and vision.
  • Mapping the ecosystem of organisations, policies, and incentives shaping the problem and solutions.
  • Creating a theory of change: the plan for allocating donations to shape the ecosystem and create envisioned outcomes.

Pillar II

Performance measurement

  • Designing research-backed KPIs, calculators, and dashboards to measure complex, long-term outcomes.
  • Collaborating with grantees to implement or strengthen data reporting processes and performance tracking.

Pillar III

Ongoing support

  • Providing regular strategy reviews as conditions evolve.
  • Updating measurement tools and reporting frameworks for continued alignment.
•Case study 01

A large national retailer with an at-the-till donation program engaged Robin to design a system to measure the impact of its charitable giving. The goal was to make more informed partnership decisions and strengthen performance conversations with funded organisations through a dashboard that balanced simplicity with analytical rigour.

Robin began by clarifying the retailer's charitable mission and refining its intended outcomes, conducting a thorough review of the program's theory of change to ensure funded activities were logically connected to measurable impact.

Building on this foundation, Robin developed a custom KPI dashboard that consolidated performance metrics into a single, defensible index reflecting overall program effectiveness. Grounded in relevant academic literature and robust quantitative methods, the framework enables the client to track progress, identify high-performing partners, and allocate capital more strategically over time.

•Case study 02

The Fort Calgary Society engaged Robin to develop a KPI dashboard to measure its progress toward both the City of Calgary's strategic directives and its own mission to educate the public about Calgary's history, strengthen community cohesion, and advance reconciliation.

Robin began by building a detailed logic model that mapped the organisation's activities to its intended outcomes. The underlying theory of change was rigorously substantiated through academic research and emerging evaluation frameworks focused on reconciliation and community-based programming.

The result was a comprehensive, evidence-based suite of KPIs that translated complex and often abstract goals, particularly reconciliation and social cohesion, into measurable indicators of institutional progress.
